About Me

My name is Andres Rodriguez.
I was born in Mexico and I moved to the United States when I was 10 years old.

I am a Junior at the University of Georgia studing Computer Science.

I started my software path when I was 14 years old, programming C# on a netbook, following a tutorial to recreate a heavily toned down version of the JARVIS artificial intelligence from the Ironman movies.
After that I learned basic Web Development at my highschool, learning HTML, CSS and some Javascript.
Shortly after I got accepted into the University of Georgia (UGA) I was accepted into the Small Satellite Reserach Lab at UGA. This laboratory has been a large part of my life since 2019 and has helped me find what I want to do with my Computer Science Major. Here is where I learned all about Flight Software pertaining to Small Satellites. I have learned so much in this laboratory and it has helped me develop my software skills and it also has allowed me to gain some hardware experience.

I will graduate from UGA the month of May 2022 and I will pursue a career in the aerospace field as a flight software engineer or an embedded software engineer.
