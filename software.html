SSRL

As of 2019, I was accepted into the UGA Small Satellite Research Laboratory (SSRL) as a member of the MOCI Electronics Team. Now the MOCI Flight Software Team.

Currently I am working on the MOCI (Multiview Onboard Computational Imager) satellite. More specifically I was tasked to develop the API for the Nvidia TX2i that will be launched on the satellite as part of its payload. I have developed this control program in Python and it successfuly commands the optical payload by taking pictures as well as transferring its own telemetry and the gathered images to the Onboard Computer (OBC) through UART.
The TX2i's main goal is to gather images and perform Structure from Motion (SfM) to generate 3D models of its targets, like Mount Everest. To do this the TX2 has different states or functions and the master program handles these programs. I was also tasked with developing a program to handle the switching back and forth between the several data processing states that the TX2i will need to have.


Most recently, in August of 2020, I was promoted to the MOCI Flight Software Team Lead. During this time the team grew to be the largest team at the SSRL and we have succesfully sped up the development of MOCI's Flight Software.
I moved on to develop the interface between our OBC and the Attitude and Determination Control System (ADCS) which will control the positon and orientation of the satellite in orbit. This component is one of largest components onboard MOCI and requires the use of I2C.
